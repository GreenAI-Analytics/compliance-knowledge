Payroll compliance in Ireland requires accurate pay calculations, timely Revenue submissions, and clear employee records.

Key points for SMEs:

- Register for PAYE with Revenue before employing staff, and use Revenue Online Service (ROS) for submissions.
- Retrieve and apply Revenue Payroll Notifications (RPNs) each pay period so tax credits and cut-off points are correct.
- Operate payroll in real time under PAYE Modernisation: submit payroll details to Revenue on or before every payment date.
- Provide itemised payslips showing gross pay, deductions (PAYE, PRSI, USC), and net pay.
- Apply statutory minimums and legal rates correctly, including National Minimum Wage and relevant PRSI classes.
- Track statutory leave and pay obligations, including annual leave, public holidays, statutory sick leave, and maternity/paternity entitlements where applicable.
- Keep records of hours, pay, deductions, and payroll submissions for the required retention period.
- Reconcile payroll records monthly against bank payments and Revenue confirmations.
- Maintain a year-end review process to validate employee totals, benefits-in-kind treatment, and any corrections.
- Document payroll responsibilities and internal checks so payroll can continue during absences or handovers.

Practical tip: maintain a payroll compliance calendar with pay dates, submission deadlines, and monthly reconciliation checkpoints.
